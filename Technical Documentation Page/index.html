Definition

Diabetes is a syndrome of disordered metabolism and hyperglycemia secondary to an absolute or relative deficiency of insulin, or a reduction in biological effectiveness of insulin, or both.

Diagnostic Criteria

To diagnose DM-2, a patient needs to have any one of the following:

  • Presence of symptoms suggestive of DM. This includes: polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia, nocturia, AND a random blood glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL)
    • OR
  • Fasting Plasma Glucose ≥ 7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL) OR
  • 2h post-prandial 75g OGTT: ≥ 11.1 mmol/L (200mg/dL) OR
  • Random plasma gucose ≥ 11.1mmol/L (200mg/dL) OR
  • HbA1c: ≥ 6.5%
How to Take a Diabetic History

It is important to establish the onset of the disease to establish protocol and determine a method of monitoring the patient.

    We must first ask:
  • When did the patient first notice their symptoms? Ask about the age of onset of symptoms, in what context (for e.g. ? pregnancy, ? ddx of DM-1 if age of onset in adolescence - early adulthood)
  • How was it diagnosed first?
  • Is it managed? What medications are being used currently? What has been prescribed in the past? Change of dose or addition of insulin?
  • Ask about compliance with diabetic diet.
  • Do they keep up with their referrals? For e.g. Opthal clinic, Renal clinic, Cardio clinic etc..
  • Any prior hospital admissions, esp for DKA/HHS?
  • Latest HbA1c?
  • Ask about ulcers present on feet, or any tingling in the extremities
Insulin Sliding Scale

Physicians use the insulin sliding scale to determine the appropriate dose of insulin an individual needs to achieve adequate glycemic control.

Most of the insulin therapy will be long acting (basal insulin) insulin. The dose is adjusted based on the patient's weight in kg.

1U insulin decreases 1mmol/L of serum glucose 70% NPH and 30% soluble insulin

Consider a female pt, 40kg with DM-2. She is currently uncontrolled. Calculate insulin needed fro glycemic control.

Using 1U per kg = 40U soluble total

Divide the soluble units into 2/3 and 1/3 doses. The 2/3 will be used for NPH throughout the day and 1/3 will be used for the soluble for the day.

2 / 3 * 40 = 26 U 1 / 3 * 40 = 14 U soluble for the day

Total NPH dose:
Convert the 2/3 soluble units into NPH by * by 2/3 again.

26 U * 2 / 3 = 18 U NPH for the day

This 18U should be administered throughtout the day, split into AM and PM doses.

Morning NPH = 18 * 2/3 = 12 U Evening NPH = 18 * 1/3 = 6 U
Macrovascular Complications of DM

Complications from DM can be either macro or microvascular. In the case of macrovascular complications, these can be:

  • Coronary Artery Disease
  • Stroke
  • PAD
  • Ulcerations to extremities
